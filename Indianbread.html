Butter Naan

Ingredients required : -

  • Maida(flour) - 400 grams ( 2 cup) + 100 grams dry Maida
  • Fresh curd - 50 grams (1/4 cup)
  • Baking soda - 1/4 tsp
  • Sugar - 1/2 tsp
  • Salt - 2/3 tsp or add to taste
  • Oil - 1 1/2 tbsp
  • Ghee/butter to make Naan

Instructions : -

  1. Filter Maida in any utensil and keep aside. Make a little crater in the middle of the Maida with your hands. Now put curd, baking soda, salt, sugar and oil in the crater and mix these properly with Maida using your hands.
  2. Put little amounts of water at a time in the mixture and knead Maida. You can feel yourself when the kneaded dough turns very soft and smooth. Cover the dough and keep it aside in a warm place for 2 hours allowing it to ferment.
  3. Heat the Tandoor(furnace).
  4. Make 10 equal sized balls of the dough. Take one ball of Maida, apply dry flour and roll into a round circle(5 to 6 inches in diameter). Spread butter/Ghee on it evenly. Fold the rolled Naan into half(forming a semi circle), then again apply butter /Ghee on this folded section. Now double this fold as it takes the shape of a triangle then apply dry flour to it. Then prepare by rolling it like a parantha on all 3 sides(thinly) and make them ready.
  5. Place this rolled Naan in the Tandoor to cook. After one side turns brown flip the Naan over to the other side. Butter Naan is ready. Take out Butter Naan on a plate and apply Ghee on it, Cut into 2 halves using a Naan cutter. Prepare all Naans in this same manner.
  6. Serve hot Butter Naan with Dal Makahni, Arhar Dal or Shahi Paneer and eat.

Tandoori Roti

Ingredients required : -

  • 2½ cup wheat flour
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ cup curd
  • water, for kneading
  • 2 tbsp oil

Instructions : -

  1. firstly, in a large bowl take 2½ cup wheat flour, 1 tsp sugar, 1 tsp baking powder, ¼ tsp baking soda and ½ tsp salt.
  2. mix well making sure everything is well combined.
  3. now add ½ cup curd and start to knead the dough.
  4. add water as required and continue to knead for 5 minutes.
  5. now add 2 tbsp oil and knead until the dough turns fdmooth and soft.
  6. cover and rest for 1 hour or more.
  7. after 1 hour, kenad the dough again making sure the dough is soft.
  8. pinch a ball sized dough and dust with wheat flour.
  9. roll gently, making sure the roti is slightly thick.
  10. now sprinkle some water and spread uniformly.
  11. heat the tawa on medium flame, and once the tawa is hot transfer the roti onto the tawa.
  12. make sure the waterside is facing down. press gently, so that the roti sticks to the pan.
  13. cook for a minute or until the roti partially puffs up and the base gets cooked up.
  14. now flip the tawa onto the flame and cook until the roti is cooked uniformly.
  15. don't worry if there are some black spots appearing. it gives a tandoor effect.
  16. scrape the roti and make sure the roti is cooked completely from all the sides.
  17. finally, brush the tandoori roti with butter and enjoy with curry.

Stuffed Paneer Kulcha

Ingredients required : -

  • Oil 5 tbsp.
  • Onions (chopped lengthwise) 4 big sized.
  • Capsicum 2 big sized.
  • Tomatoes 5 medium-sized
  • Tomato Sauce 2 tbsp.
  • Mayonnaise 4-5 tbsp
  • Maggi Masala 2 tbsp.
  • Paneer 400 grams.
  • Chilli Flakes 1 tbsp.
  • Oreganos 1 tbsp.
  • Soya sauce 1 tsp.
  • White vinegar 1 tsp.

Instructions : -

  1. In a kadai or deep pan, heat oil. Add onions. Fry it till it gets translucent. (lightly brown).
  2. Add chopped capsicum and cook it till it gets soft.
  3. Now add chopped tomatoes. Cook it for approx 5 minutes.
  4. Add tomato sauce, mayonnaise and Maggi masala to the mixture and mix well. Feel free to add your favorite spice instead of Maggi masala.
  5. Add Soya sauce and white vinegar and mix it well.
  6. Add paneer cubes in the mixture and mix it well.
  7. Sprinkle chilli flakes, oreganos and chopped coriander leaves. Mix well.
  8. Take one kulcha and add Paneer mixture on one half and add salsa sauce or any other sauce / chutney on the other half.
  9. Grease both sides and grill the kulcha for 7-10 mins.
  10. Your Paneer stuff kulcha is now ready. Serve it with tea, coffee or butter milk.

Tandoori Garlic Butter Naan

Ingredients required : -

  • 1 Cup Wheat flour
  • 1 tbsp Butter
  • 1 tbsp Ginger
  • 2 tbsp Oil
  • 5-6 Chopped garlic
  • 3 Chopped green chillies
  • to taste Salt

Instructions : -

  1. Add the wheat flour, salt, oil and knead the dough with water.
  2. Let the dough rest for 30 minutes.
  3. In a small katori, add butter and heat it on low flame. To this add ginger, chillies and chopped garlic.
  4. Take one part of your dough and roll it out.
  5. Add the butter mixture on your roti and make sure to cover the entire base.
  6. Close and roll the roti once again.
  7. Then on a hot pan bake the tandoori garlic roti. Serve with your favourite main curry dish and enjoy!

Kashmiri Naan

Ingredients required : -

  • 150g (1 cup) cashews, crushed
  • 65g (⅔ cup) glazed cherries,finely chopped
  • 50g (½ cup) ground almonds
  • 25g (1 tbsp) honey
  • 900g (7 cups) flour
  • 15ml (1 tbsp) salt
  • 30ml (2 tbsp) baking powder
  • 300ml (1 ¼ cups) milk
  • 7g (2 ½ tsp) dried yeast
  • 30ml (2 tbsp) sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • 270g (1 ¼ cups) Greek yogurt
  • oil, for frying
  • 45ml (3 tbsp) ghee, melted

Instructions : -

    For the Filling :
  1. Place all the ingredients in a bowl and stir until everything is well incorporated. Set aside.
    2. For the Naan:
  2. Sift the flour, salt and baking powder into a large bowl. Gently heat the milk in a saucepan until warm to the touch. Don’t boil it! Pour the milk in a large bowl and whisk in the yeast and sugar.
  3. Cover the bowl of milk mixture with a cloth and keep in a warm place for 30 minutes. Ideally, it should foam.
  4. Beat the eggs and yogurt together. Pour the milk mixture into the flour, plus the egg mixture. Stir until well combined.
  5. Tip the dough out onto a clean, flour-dusted work surface and knead until the dough is soft and sticky, which should take around 10 minutes. Alternatively, you can use a stand mixer to do the job.
  6. Place the dough in a greased bowl and cover it with a cloth. Wait for at least 1 hour, but no more than 24 hours, for the dough to rise.
    7. Filling the Naan:
  7. Divide the dough into 10 balls. (Use extra flour for dusting purposes to get this part of the recipe done. Things can get sticky!) Roll out each of the 10 portions of dough balls into thick circles.
  8. Fill half of each circle with one portion of the filling leaving about an inch around the edge. Wet the dough around the edges with a little water and fold each circle in half to enclose the filling. Pinch the dough around the edges to close.
  9. Gently roll out each naan into a round shape. Shake off excess flour.
    10. Cooking the Naan:
  10. Heat a dry frying pan over a high heat. When very hot, place a naan in it.
  11. Check the bottom regularly to ensure it doesn’t burn. What you’re looking for is a golden sheen with a brown toasted look. If it starts to look too dark, turn it over to cook on the other side. The average cooking time for a naan is between 3 and 5 minutes. If you prefer your naan more charred, finish it off under a grill.
  12. Place the naan on a plate and brush with ghee. Serve immediately with your favourite Indian curry. Enjoy!
